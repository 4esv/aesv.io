I am a Mexican American generalist in my mid-twenties, based in Ithaca, New York. I work as a systems integrator at Cornell's College of Human Ecology. The rest of the week I am the sysadmin for a small tow shop, a volunteer first responder, a casual pianist and bassist, and the on-call mechanic for a small fleet of family cars.

I work across domains because the unifying interest is **connecting things** rather than perfecting any one of them.

## What I do at work

I make system A talk to system B. Concretely: forms and workflows on Frevvo, asset-management plumbing across Jamf and Snipe-IT, RBAC dashboards backed by LDAP and Shibboleth, AI integrations into research hardware. The detailed case studies live at [work.aesv.io](https://work.aesv.io).

## What I do outside work

**First response.** I run with the Enfield Volunteer Fire Company. Certified CFR, BLS, EVOC, hazmat awareness. The training is humbling and the call volume keeps you sharp.

**Mechanics.** I keep the family fleet on the road. Diagnostics, repairs, full opinions on brake fluid.

**Music.** Piano and bass mostly, drums in emergencies. Playlist ranges from corridos to Norwegian death metal — there is no through-line, and I have stopped looking for one.

**Cooking.** I prefer the long fermentations. Poolish, sourdough, slow doughs that make the kitchen smell right.

**Genetics.** I ran my own raw genotype through admixture, PCA against 1000 Genomes, and polygenic risk scores from published GWAS. Not for a result — for the pipeline.

## What this site is

A garden, in the [digital-garden](https://maggieappleton.com/garden-history) sense. Some entries are fiction, some are research, some are notes I want to be able to find later. Things change here. Some pages are unfinished on purpose.

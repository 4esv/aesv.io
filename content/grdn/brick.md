And so I pulled the brick as if it were a pull cord, with so much energy it broke past weightless and felt as if it wanted to float away taking me with it. But then, it grew heavy again and in a twitch I was tensing every fiber I could muster into pulling it down. It smeared more than obliterate, the nose took the brunt but his orbital still gave away like ripe fruit. Instinctively I repeated the motion, again and again, with each pulping the brick grew heavier until the exhaustion and the scene before me captivated me. There, in the stillness and quiet, there was an odd peace and a sense of bewilderment. I couldn't believe that bowl of teeth and mucus was once a face.
